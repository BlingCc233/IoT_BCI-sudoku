\begin{englishabstract}
Brain-computer interface (BCI) IoT links carry highly sensitive neural signals and control commands, and face compound risks such as replay, tampering, man-in-the-middle attacks, and traffic fingerprinting. In response to the protocol design difficulties jointly caused by constrained devices, public-network reachability, and recognizable traffic appearance, this thesis implements and validates a composite secure communication protocol for BCI-IoT.

In terms of method, this thesis builds a protocol enhancement layer between the application layer and TCP. The handshake stage adopts certificate signatures, ephemeral key agreement, digest binding, and confirmation codes; the session stage adopts AEAD record encapsulation; the appearance layer adopts reversible byte mapping and randomized length masking, forming a configurable trade-off between the full mode and the 6-bit segmented mode. To ensure rigorous evaluation, this thesis sets up three types of experiments: local comparison, fingerprinting and active attacks, and cross-region restricted-network testing. Multiple rounds of measurements are conducted under unified metric definitions.

The experimental results show that the proposed scheme maintains latency in the same order as mainstream secure protocols under restricted public-network conditions, while showing advantages in data-plane memory usage. In security validation, the scheme stably intercepts replay and tampered inputs. In fingerprinting resistance, randomized length masking significantly reduces the class recovery rate based on length sequences.

This work presents a communication-layer protocol prototype for BCI-IoT and a reproducible experimental framework. The results show that randomized length masking reduces the class recovery rate of passive fingerprinting attacks from 100\% to 16.67\%; the cost is higher byte-stream overhead, which lowers bandwidth utilization. The findings can provide a reference for engineering selection and future standardization of BCI-IoT secure communication protocols.

\end{englishabstract}
